Types of Psoriasis


  • Scalp Psoriasis

  • Plaque Psoriasis

  • Guttate Psoriasis

  • Pustular Psoriasis

  • Nail Psoriasis

Scalp Psoriasis

Scalp Psoriasis

It is commonly seen in people who have developed Skin Psoriasis. It may leave the scalp dry and make it flaky, while the plaque peels out in clusters.

Plaque Psoriasis

Plaque Psoriasis

It is the most common form of Psoriasis which appears on the skin as red patches that are covered with dead skin cells called scales.

Guttate Psoriasis

Guttate Psoriasis

It is seen as small, round spots called papules that are raised and sometimes scaly. Guttate lesions tend to show up on the arms, legs and torso and rarely on the scalp, face or ears.

Pustular Psoriasis

Pustular Psoriasis

It is characterized by white pustules on the skin that are filled with pus, as the name suggests. The skin that surrounds the itchy and flaky lesion turns red. It may affect the areas of the body that are isolated like the hands and feet or other parts of the skin.

Nail Psoriasis

Nail Psoriasis

Psoriasis can also affect the nails in some cases. The appearance of nails may be altered and seen as small, discolored pits or large yellow colored separations on the nail plate.
